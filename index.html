About Me

I am an astrophysicist with a background in observational cosmology, systematics mitigation, and custom pipeline development. My work focuses on extracting weak signals from noisy imaging data, evaluating instrument limitations, and designing tools that support scientific missions with both precision and adaptability. I am particularly interested in interdisciplinary data challenges in space science.

Research Interests

  • Development and optimisation of custom pipelines to manage instrumental systematics to maximise scientific return, including the design and assessment of trade-offs between resolution, coverage, and signal quality.
  • Statistical signal extraction from complex, noisy, multi-wavelength data, particularly in large-scale surveys and operationally constrained environments.
  • Application of Bayesian inference and cross-correlation techniques for weak signal enhancement and structural analysis.
  • Design of automated, scalable data workflows supporting reproducibility and efficiency in science operations.
  • Ongoing interest in developing of ad-hoc and responsive methods to enhance subsurface feature detection, e.g. through cross-correlation and stacking of multi-pass radargrams.

Projects

  • Circumgalactic Dust Extinction in KiDS-DR4: Developed a Python pipeline to detect and model faint circumgalactic dust extinction signals using multi-wavelength survey data. Included systematic modelling with MICE2 simulation, trade-off analysis between photometric accuracy and sample size, and covariance analysis.
  • Detection Bias in Cosmic Shear: Created a survey-independent detection probability function for galaxies using GalSim and Source Extractor, to quantify detection bias from blending in cosmic shear measurements.
  • Near-IR Imaging of Radio Galaxy Hosts with LBT : Performed scientific data reduction of near-IR images of six radio galaxy hosts at different redshifts taken with LBT and adaptive optics, including flat-fielding, and background subtraction with IRAF, which is followed by a morphological analysis with GALFIT
